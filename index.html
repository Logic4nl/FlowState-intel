Where the volume actually lives

Chain-by-chain analysis of C1 pool opportunity, with sell-side reality check and token-segment honesty.

15 April 2026 · Source: DefiLlama 30d

Methodology

Data collection & definitions

Total DEX volume: 30-day rolling sum of all on-chain swap volume on each chain, from DefiLlama. Includes all DEX protocols (Uniswap, Curve, Balancer, etc) but excludes CEX volume.

Aggregator-routed volume: Volume that flows through one of the top aggregators on each chain (e.g. Jupiter on Solana, KyberSwap on Ethereum). Estimated from public routing data and on-chain logs.

C1 addressable: The slice of aggregator volume that represents tokens under $5M market cap. This is the core target for C1 pools. Note: we lack granular token-tier data, so this is estimated as a percentage of aggregator flow.

Sell-side channel: Any partner or platform through which FlowState can distribute liquidity to token projects. This report re-assesses which channels actually serve sub-$5M MC tokens.

Four chains, four playbooks

Ethereum: the volume fortress

$20.56B aggregator-routed volume in 30d. But this includes all tokens. KyberSwap leads at 26.9%, followed by CoWSwap (16.3%), 1inch (10.2%), Bebop (8.1%), and 0x (7.9%). The Ethereum narrative is often "DeFi maturity" but the reality for sub-$5M tokens is fragmented: long-tail tokens route through whichever aggregator offers the best execution, which often depends on liquidity depth and fee structure. FlowState's play: integrate with KyberSwap first (highest volume), then evaluate CoWSwap (second tier but strong for complex orders).

Key insight: KyberSwap alone can unlock ~$5.5B of the $20.56B. But this includes mega-cap tokens. The true sub-$5M addressable is much smaller. Set expectations accordingly.

Solana: Jupiter's 70% monopoly

$14.55B aggregator-routed. Jupiter captures 67.5% — a near-monopoly. Titan (11.9%), OKX (9.4%), and DFlow (7.6%) split the rest. The Solana sub-$5M token ecosystem is massive (memecoin launches daily) but short-lived (72-hour average lifespan). FlowState's play: Jupiter integration is table-stakes. The exit liquidity C1 provides matters most for tokens that graduate from pump.fun but haven't reached CEX listing — that's the sweet spot where Jupiter routing finds real value in C1 pools.

Key insight: Jupiter volume looks huge, but the churn-filtered addressable (tokens older than 7 days, under $5M MC) is a fraction of the headline number. But it's still the highest-concentration, best-execution opportunity for C1.

BSC: LiquidMesh dominance and the OKX path

$6.07B aggregator-routed. LiquidMesh (38.7%) is wallet-integrated (Trust Wallet, Binance Web3 Wallet) and proprietary — hard to access directly. But OKX Swap is #2 at 18.8%, and OKX integration is already in FlowState's pipeline. That opens ~$1.1B of BSC's addressable. 1inch (11.8%) and Binance Wallet (11.4%) are next. BSC is harder than Ethereum but not a write-off. The realistic path: pursue OKX, then 1inch.

Key insight: Don't try to crack LiquidMesh (Binance ecosystem-gated). Pursue OKX for BSC volume. OKX integration as a side-win to Ethereum TGE.

Base: OpenOcean and the aggregator layer

$4.46B aggregator-routed. OpenOcean leads at 35.2% — a dominant position. 0x (12.4%), OKX (9.7%), KyberSwap (9.2%), and Velora (4.9%) follow. Base is an emerging chain with growing long-tail token activity. FlowState's play: OpenOcean integration is essential. The platform serves all-token-sizes including sub-$5M. KyberSwap is already integrated (Ethereum) so Base reuse is cheap. But OpenOcean is the primary channel.

Key insight: Base is Priority 2 (after Ethereum). OpenOcean must be first integration. Ethereum TGE should land first; Base secondary wave summer 2026.

Plasma and the watchlist

Plasma: asymmetric upside bet

Stablecoin-focused L1. $0.30B total DEX, $0.21B addressable at 68.7% aggregator share. KyberSwap routes 84% of Plasma's aggregated volume. Deployment is trivial (KyberSwap already integrated). The ask: why Plasma? Not for revenue today ($2.1M annual at 1% fee on full addressable). The asymmetry: Plasma's unlock schedule (2.5B XPL in July 2026) could crash the token, or it could signal confidence and attract ecosystem builders. If Plasma grows 10x, FlowState is already positioned at near-zero cost. The risk/reward is legitimately good. Maintain as a no-cost deployment option.

Key insight: 2.5B XPL unlock is real risk. But deployment cost is so low that even 1% upside probability justifies it. Keep Plasma on the roadmap as a side-win post-Ethereum TGE.

Monad: watch and react

Growing fast (+22% aggregator volume month-over-month). $1.64B total DEX, $0.24B addressable, KyberSwap at 84%. High-performance L1 thesis is compelling but execution is still unproven. The play: don't invest in Monad-specific BD now. Wait for Q3 2026 (network stability proof). If growth continues, KyberSwap deployment handles routing automatically. Revisit post-summer.

Other chains (Arbitrum, Polygon, Avalanche, Sui):

All below $2B addressable. Pursue integrations opportunistically (via multi-chain aggregator partnerships like 1inch) but don't prioritize dedicated BD. These are tail traffic, not core.

Sell-side: the honest picture

Tier-1 MMs (Wintermute, GSR, Cumberland, Jump, DWF, Flowdesk, B2C2, Keyrock) primarily serve tokens above $50M MC. They're outside our segment. Below, each sell-side channel is assessed with a strict filter: does this counterparty actually trade tokens under $5M MC?

Reality check

There is no identified market maker that explicitly uses oracle-priced DEX liquidity pools as an exit venue for sub-$5M tokens. The sell-side won't come from tier-1 MMs. It will come from token project teams (via Series X) and individual holders (via unlock trackers).

The sell-side MM reality

There is no identified market maker that uses DEX liquidity pools as an exit venue for sub-$5M tokens. Institutional MMs serve large-cap. When presented with a $3M MC token, they widen spreads to 20%+ or decline. Deprioritize the institutional MM channel.

4. Crypto OTC desks

OTC desks serve $50M+ tokens

Low fit for sub-$5M

When presented with a $3M MC token, they widen spreads to 20%+ or decline. FlowState's pitch (fail-safe long-tail liquidity) is conceptually right but practically, OTC desks don't handle enough sub-$5M volume.

Deprioritize.

5. Crypto VCs

VC engagement path

VCs won't engage with FlowState directly until there's traction. The realistic path: via portfolio companies. When a portfolio company uses Series X for vesting, the exit-via-C1 path becomes natural. This is the wedge to unlock VC channel buy-in.

6. DAO treasuries

DAO liquidity co-seeding is a legitimate channel for brand partnerships and long-tail token discovery. Lower priority due to heavy BD effort and low frequency.

Known gaps in this analysis

Token-tier volume breakdown

We don't have per-token MC-tier data for aggregator-routed volume. The headline $51B addressable includes ALL tokens routed through aggregators, but FlowState only serves sub-$5M MC. The actual addressable is likely 1-5% of the headline. This is the single most important missing number. Recommendation: pursue DefiLlama or Dune API for token-level volume granularity in next analysis cycle.

Sell-side quantification

We know sell-side channels conceptually but have no data on actual sell volume from vesting unlocks for sub-$5M tokens. Recommendation: aggregate unlock event data (Tokenomist, CryptoRank) for Q1 2026 to estimate unlock-triggered sell volume baseline.

Competitor pricing

No data on pricing other liquidity venues offer for sub-$5M MC tokens. Comparison would strengthen the C1 value proposition. Recommendation: model competitor pricing (CurveAMM, 0x liquidity sources) to quantify FlowState's fee advantage.

Bottom line

Buy-side: Ethereum remains the largest chain by aggregator-routed volume ($20.6B/30d). But FlowState's actual addressable is a fraction of this — the sub-$5M MC slice. The headline numbers tell us where to deploy, not how much revenue to expect. Solana is co-priority via Jupiter. Base and BSC are secondary, post-Ethereum TGE.

Sell-side: Tier-1 MMs serve large-cap. Vesting platforms serve large-cap. The realistic sell-side channels are: (1) token project teams using Series X, (2) individual holders via unlock trackers, (3) boutique platforms (Hedgey, Sablier) if we target their long-tail users. Deprioritize institutional MMs and OTC desks.

Watchlist: Plasma play unchanged (asymmetric, zero-cost). Monad growing (+22% agg volume) and already covered via KyberSwap. Other chains are tail traffic.

Critical gap: Token-tier volume data. Without per-token MC breakdowns, the headline addressable numbers are directionally useful but magnitude-uncertain. Pursue granular token-level data in next cycle.
